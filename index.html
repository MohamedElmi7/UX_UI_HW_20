UX Mobile Project:  Smol

Discover a new and easier way to support your local small businesses. With Smol, small business shoppers can find and follow their new favorite small businesses while also staying in the know about sales, hot drops, restocks, and more events hosted by small businesses in their area.

Project overview

The Problem: Many small business shoppers are not aware of the products and services offered by small businesses in their area. They lack the means to easily follow small businesses as well as to stay up to date with product availability across different small businesses. How might we enable small business shoppers to easily find and follow small businesses in the area, while giving them visibility to product availability, quality, source, and pricing information in order to encourage more small business shopping and support the local economy?

The Solution: Smol is an app that enables & facilitates users to discover and follow small businesses in their area, giving them access to product information such as availability, source, and pricing.

User Research

Interview & Surveys

We started by conducting 5 user interviews and collecting 20 survey responses in order to discover users’ considerations and preferences when shopping with small businesses. We discovered there was an opportunity to create a solution for business and product visibility.

Key Insights & Results

Overall, What we discovered from our interviews & surveys is that the majority of the pain points that people faced when shopping with small businesses is not being sure, whether or not the products will be available.As well as favoring quality despite the price. From this insight, we were able to determine that there was an opportunity to create a solution for business and product visibility.

Definition & Synthesis

Affinity Map

After being able to gather data from our interviews and surveys we were able to develop an affinity diagram.

User Persona

After compiling with and organizing our data we were able to create the user persona based on our User insight.

User insight: Users like to browse small businesses for unique experiences and high-quality products. However, lack of product availability can discourage them from shopping small as frequently. We want to encourage users to feel like regulars by shopping at small businesses.

Competitor Analysis

According to our survey results, some of the other apps that solve the problem of product visibility include Google Maps, Etsy, TikTok, and Instagram. Our competitor analysis provided the insights on how they compare and contrast to our solution.

Ideation

Feature Prioritization Matrix

We had a wide variety of features and ideas for the app but we need to be clear and concise with our approach, so we utilized a Feature Prioritization Matrix to organize/ decide what we wanted to implement.

User Scenario

Once we had the features we wanted we wanted to be able to visualize how and when would a user use our application. So we created a User scenario based on our user persona who is looking for a gift for their partner.

User Flow

For our user flow, we decided that after onboarding, the user would come to an intro survey. After the intro survey, they’ll land on a For You feed, where they can then navigate to product detail and business profile pages.

User Testing & Outcomes

User Test Plan

We tested our prototype on 4 users. Our main objectives where to see if users were able to discover businesses then get relevant product information AND if users are able to navigate to the alerts page and get directions to a business that they follow.

Conclusion & Future Opportunities

Conclusion

Our user testing provided valuable feedback. Our flows and tasks were all successfully completed by user testers, though further improvements around design elements and building out the rest of the features would improve the functionality and vision of smol.

Final thoughts

- With more time we would build out the “sign in with Google” option along with other social sign ups. - This will make the login/sign up process more efficient for our users.   - We were asked, “How would I return to the intro survey if I wanted to update my selections?” We didn’t initially build this into the prototype, but given more time we would build out this ability under the profile in the bottom navigation.   - With more time we would build out the following tab and the elements in the navigation bar including: profile, favorites and maps. We would also add more design elements (color, etc.)
